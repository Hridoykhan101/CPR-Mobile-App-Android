About This Application

The purpose of this application is to serve as an academic training tool for teaching the general public about CPR. In Australia, around 30,000 people may suffer a sudden cardiac arrest each year. Out of these 30,000 people, only around 9-10% survive.

The goal of this application is to teach those uninformed about cardiac arrest, and to ensure that signs of a cardiac arrest are prevented in the first place by recognising the symptoms.

Symptoms of Cardiac arrest include:

  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeats
  • Shortness of breath
  • Lightheadedness/dizziness where the affected person may feel like fainting

By teaching signs of recognition and immediate CPR training, we can increase the survival rate of those who suffer cardiac arrest.
