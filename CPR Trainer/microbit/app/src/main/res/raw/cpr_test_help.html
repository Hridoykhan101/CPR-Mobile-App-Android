CPR TEST

Ideal Results

Users should aim to achieve the following stats to perform adequate compression training:
- 100 to 120 CPM (Compression per Minute).
- 80% or higher compression fraction.
- 1/3 of the chest depth (5cm in Average).

How to use:

- CPR TEST is where you are able to conduct CPR training to mimic a real life scenario.

- The Gauge will indicate how deep you compress alongside a depth measurement.

- An indicator measuring "SPEED UP, GOOD, or SLOW DOWN" will relate to your compression rate.

- The Rate will indicate the speed as to which you are performing compressions.

- The Depth will indicate how deep you are currently performing compressions.

- The Compressions will indicate how many compressions you have completed, whether they be complete or not.

- The Complete Compressions will indicate how many full compressions you have completed.

- Select the "Restart Test" option to restart the timer alongside the visible measurements.

- The set time is directly underneath the gauge. This can be changed in the 'settings' tab.
